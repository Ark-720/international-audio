永樂款銅鎏金度母像
English
中文
S003

明朝皇室中設有專門製作藏式佛像的宮廷御用監，宮廷造像主要作為賞賜給西藏宗教領袖之禮，呈現出漢藏合璧、紋飾絢麗的新風貌，見證了明代漢藏民族之間的友好關係和頻繁的文化交流，在歷史、工藝和宗教上都具有極為重要的意義。

這尊銅鎏金佛像為明代宮廷造像的典範之作，工藝風格成熟精細，展現出雍容華貴的氣度。度母也稱為「多羅菩薩」，此像髮髻高盤，頭戴五花冠，大耳垂肩，胸前佩戴連珠瓔珞，腰間前後都有連珠紋裝飾，雙層蓮花座的蓮瓣造型對列工整，瓣頭以卷草紋裝飾，台座上由左至右陰刻楷書「大明永樂年施」六字款。當我們360度旋轉佛像時，可以看到台座底下的封底已缺失，露出佛像十分規整的內膛以及黃銅質地，在佛像法象威儀的完美造型之外，讓我們得以一窺古代佛像鑄造技術之堂奧。

Gilt bronze figure of the bodhisattva Tara
English
中文
S003

In the Ming period, the royal house established a special bureau to produce Tibetan Buddha statues. A synthesis of Chinese and Tibetan styles, the works were usually presented to Tibetan religious leaders, promoting harmony between the Han Chinese and Tibetan peoples. The new style of ornamentation bears witness to the friendly relations and frequent cultural exchanges between the Han and Tibetans during the Ming Dynasty, which were of great historical, artistic, and religious significance.

This gilt bronze figure of the bodhisattva Tara, exemplifies Ming Dynasty Buddhist statuary at its finest, the craftsmanship mature and meticulous, imparting an air of richness and grace. The Tara’s hair is arranged in a high coil. On her head is a five-panel crown. Her earlobes hang to her shoulders, an elaborate necklace rests on her chest, and decorative tassels dangle at the front and back of her waist. The double-tiered lotus pedestal on which she sits is neatly aligned, and the tips of the lotus petals are adorned with scroll-grass motifs. On the pedestal, six Chinese characters reading "Made in the Yongle reign of the great Ming dynasty" are engraved in regular script from left to right. When we rotate the image 360 degrees, we see that the bottom cover is missing, revealing the statue’s interior structure and metallic texture. In addition to representing a perfectly formed, stately Buddhist figure, the work also gives viewers a glimpse into the inner recesses of ancient copper-casting technology.
